Climate Analysis

The Rise of CO₂ and Global Temperature

A Data-Driven Study of Industrial Acceleration

Research Question

How did CO₂ emissions change during and after industrialization, and how strongly are these changes associated with global temperature rise?

Understanding Global Warming

Global warming refers to the long-term rise in Earth’s average surface temperature. Scientists measure temperature anomaly, which represents deviation from a historical baseline.

The Industrial Era

Beginning around 1750, industrialization introduced large-scale fossil fuel combustion, leading to a dramatic increase in atmospheric carbon emissions.

Rise of CO₂ Emissions

Emissions remained low prior to industrialization but accelerated sharply after 1950, marking the period of rapid industrial growth.

Temperature Acceleration

Global temperature anomalies show a clear upward acceleration, increasing over four times faster after 1950 compared to earlier periods.

Statistical Relationship

  • Correlation: 0.815
  • R²: 0.6646
  • Post-1950 warming rate: 4x higher

Conclusion

The analysis demonstrates a strong statistical relationship between industrial CO₂ emissions and accelerated global temperature rise.
